Everest Camp Trek

About Everest Camp Trek

The Everest Camp Trek is a breathtaking adventure that takes you to the base camp of the world's highest mountain, Mount Everest. This trek is a once-in-a-lifetime experience for trekkers and nature enthusiasts seeking to witness the majestic Himalayan peaks and experience the unique Sherpa culture.

The trek begins in Lukla, a small town with an airport in the Khumbu region of Nepal. From Lukla, trekkers make their way through picturesque landscapes, lush forests, and charming Sherpa villages, each step bringing them closer to the iconic Everest Base Camp.

The journey also includes visits to ancient monasteries, like the Tengboche Monastery, and offers panoramic views of mountains like Ama Dablam, Lhotse, and Nuptse. Along the way, trekkers can enjoy warm hospitality at teahouses and lodges, making it a comfortable yet adventurous expedition.

Route

The Everest Camp Trek typically takes around 12 to 14 days to complete, depending on the trekker's pace and acclimatization needs. The route follows the following key points:

  • Day 1: Fly to Lukla (2,800m) and trek to Phakding (2,652m)
  • Day 2: Trek to Namche Bazaar (3,440m)
  • Day 3: Acclimatization day at Namche Bazaar
  • Day 4: Trek to Tengboche (3,867m)
  • Day 5: Trek to Dingboche (4,410m)
  • Day 6: Acclimatization day at Dingboche
  • Day 7: Trek to Lobuche (4,910m)
  • Day 8: Trek to Everest Base Camp (5,364m) and back to Gorak Shep (5,164m)
  • Day 9: Hike to Kala Patthar (5,545m) and descend to Pheriche (4,240m)
  • Day 10: Trek to Namche Bazaar
  • Day 11: Trek to Lukla
  • Day 12: Fly back to Kathmandu
